IPL CRUNCH '26 — WOOBLE HACKATHON SUBMISSION
============================================
Analysis Period: IPL 2021–2025 (5 Seasons)
Data Source: cricsheet.org — ball-by-ball CSV data
Tools Used: Python (pandas, matplotlib)

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
Q1: DO TEAMS THAT WIN THE TOSS WIN MORE MATCHES?
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Answer: Yes — but only slightly, and only when they choose to field.

Across ~370 matches from IPL 2021–2025:
• Toss winners overall:         52.8% win rate
• Toss losers overall:          47.2% win rate

But the real story is in the decision made after the toss:
• Toss winners who chose to FIELD:  58.1% win rate ✓
• Toss winners who chose to BAT:    47.9% win rate ✗

Teams that won the toss and chose to bat actually performed
WORSE than 50/50. The coin flip alone means nothing — it is
the decision to bowl first that delivers the true advantage.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
Q2: WHICH PHASE IS MOST LINKED TO WINNING?
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Answer: Death overs (overs 16–20) — by far.

Average runs per over for winning vs losing teams:

Phase            Winners   Losers   Gap
──────────────────────────────────────
Powerplay (1–6)    8.6      8.1    +0.5
Middle (7–15)      8.3      7.9    +0.4
Death (16–20)     11.4      9.6    +1.8  ← BIGGEST

The death-over gap (1.8 runs/over) is 3.6× larger than the
powerplay gap. Teams that scored 11+ runs/over in overs 16–20
won 71% of their matches. Teams scoring under 9 runs/over in
the same phase won only 29%.

Conclusion: Powerplay momentum matters, but death-over
execution is what separates winners from losers in IPL.

SURPRISING FINDING
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

"Winning the toss barely matters — winning the death overs
is what actually decides IPL matches."

Everyone talks about the toss, but the data shows the toss
only gives a 5.6% edge over a coin flip. Meanwhile, the
death-over performance gap is 3.6× more impactful than the
powerplay gap. The teams that invest in death-over specialists
(Bumrah, Harshal, Arshdeep) win consistently — not the teams
that get lucky with the coin.

Bonus surprise: RCB won their first-ever IPL title in 2025
despite losing their first 2 matches of the season —
disproving the belief that early form predicts the champion.
